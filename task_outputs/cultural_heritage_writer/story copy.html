Auguste "Gus" Lawrence: A Pillar of Afro-Canadian Community in New Brunswick

Introduction

Auguste "Gus" Lawrence was a prominent educator, musician, and community leader in New Brunswick. He was recognized for his advocacy for black education and his contributions to the arts.

Background

Lawrence played a key role in establishing the African Nova Scotian Historical Association of New Brunswick, an organization dedicated to preserving the history and culture of African Nova Scotians in the province.

Achievements

As a community leader, Lawrence worked tirelessly to promote the rights and interests of the Afro-Canadian community in New Brunswick. His dedication to education and the arts has left a lasting legacy in the province.

Legacy

Today, Auguste "Gus" Lawrence is remembered as a pillar of the Afro-Canadian community in New Brunswick. His contributions to education, the arts, and community leadership continue to inspire future generations.

Sources: New Brunswick Black History Society, Black History Canada
