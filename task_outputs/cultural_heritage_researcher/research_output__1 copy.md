## Candidate 2: Anna Minerva Henderson
**Candidate_info**
- **Name**: Anna Minerva Henderson
- **Background**: Born in 1863, Henderson was an influential educator and poet. She became the first Black female principal in New Brunswick and fought for educational reforms benefiting Afro-Canadian communities.
- **Cultural Context**: Henderson’s legacy as a pioneer in education represents the historical struggle for equality and access to knowledge among Black Canadians.

## Candidate 3: Willie O'Ree
**Candidate_info**
- **Name**: Willie O'Ree
- **Background**: Born in 1935, O'Ree broke racial barriers as the first Black player in the NHL. He faced immense challenges and persisted to establish a career in hockey.
- **Cultural Context**: O'Ree's success in sports made significant impacts on diversity and representation in hockey, serving as a role model for aspiring athletes.

## Candidate 8: Mary Ann Shadd
**Candidate_info**
- **Name**: Mary Ann Shadd
- **Background**: Achieving fame as the first Black woman to publish a newspaper in North America, Shadd dedicated her life to journalism and activism for abolition.
- **Cultural Context**: Her influence extended beyond journalism; she laid the groundwork for future generations of activists and journalists contributing to civil rights.
