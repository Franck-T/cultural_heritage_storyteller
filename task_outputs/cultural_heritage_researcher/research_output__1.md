# Afro-Canadian Notable Figures in New Brunswick

## 1. Abraham Beverley Walker
**Candidate Info**:   
- **Name**: **Abraham Beverley Walker**  
- **Background**: Born in 1851, he was the first Black lawyer in New Brunswick and a significant figure in civil rights activism. Walker fought for equal rights and was pivotal in establishing the legal framework for the rights of African Canadians.  
- **Cultural Context**: As a trailblazer for Black lawyers in Canada, Walker's legacy influences ongoing discussions surrounding social justice and equality.

## 2. Measha Brueggergosman
**Candidate Info**:  
- **Name**: **Measha Brueggergosman**  
- **Background**: A celebrated soprano born in 1977 in Fredericton, she is known for her powerful voice and contributions to classical music. She emerged from local church choirs and gained international acclaim.

## 3. Willie O'Ree
**Candidate Info**:  
- **Name**: **Willie O'Ree**  
- **Background**: Born in 1935 in Fredericton, O'Ree broke racial barriers as the first Black player in the NHL in 1958. He has been a significant cultural icon in sports, advocating for diversity across athletics.  
- **Cultural Context**: O'Ree's achievements embody the fight against racial discrimination in professional sports.

**Multimedia References**:

## 4. Lena O'Ree
**Candidate Info**:  
- **Name**: **Lena O'Ree**  
- **Background**: A Civil Rights activist born in 1914 in Saint John, she was the first Black radio show host in Canada, using her platform to advocate for the rights of the Black community.  
- **Cultural Context**: Her contributions to the media paved the way for enhanced visibility and representation of Black voices.
